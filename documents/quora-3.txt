The CCNA certification exam is the basic subject in Cisco certification exams. Generally, the certification exam is very easy to pass. but, as for a fresher, it also is difficult to pass. Here, I will share some of my study methods to prepare for the CCNA Routing and Switching exam.

Decide the Schedule of your Exam

The journey begins with the question, which CCNA exam should you take first? Most people start with CCNA RS. The typical progression is ROUTE, SWITCH, and finally, TSHOOT, but you can make the first two in any order. According to general, recommend that you take the exam you are most confident you’ll pass with the most ease. If you’re in the industry already, and your day-to-day work is switch-heavy, then you might switch the first two exams, taking SWITCH first. Passing the first one (whichever you choose) gets you to the second, and boosts your confidence. And confidence is essential when you’re walking into an exam.

Get lots of hands-on experience

You’ll see the right mix of theoretical and practical in the CCNA Routing and Switching exams, so you should get as much hands-on experience as possible. Cisco recommends that you should already have one to three years of experience with Cisco products to sit for the exam, which means you might have done the tasks before. On the reviews, however, you’re timed and tested. That makes a big difference psychologically. Hands-on experience not only provides strong validation of the technical information. It also helps you overcome the psychological element. Hands-on experience is such a big part of the exams that allows the candidates to ensure that they are well prepared for the simulation portions of the reviews.

Utilize a blend of additional resources

when I prepared for the exam, my friends recommended SPOTO. because SPOTO provides a personal scheduled study plan. my friends passed the exam on the first try. so I want to recommend it for you.

Relax

And then go out and conquer the CCNA Routing and Switching exams one at a time.

How is the CCNA exam?
Profile photo for Rakibul Islam Salafi
Rakibul Islam Salafi
 · 
Follow
IT Engineer at Mumbai, India5y
The exam is ‘proctored’ which basically means you are watched by a camera.

You need to drop all your stuff in a locked box before the exam, so no phones, wallets, keys, and so on.

When you arrive you sign in, have your picture taken and sign an exam form.
You get a printed copy of your results and % on each study area when you leave as well as you need to sign out.

For the certification, you get 2 pieces of laminated paper and a non-permanent ink pen for notes or calculations.

No food/drinks in the exam room, some let you have water outside.

Click here, to see how the exam looks.
You have an introduction to how the exam works first, think 10–15 minutes.
I would use some of that time to write down useful stuff (subnets, routing, etc) comes to mind, your brain will be fried later, writing it down earlier has helped me a lot.

The CCNA normally has 40 questions and 90 minutes to answer them, if you are not an English native speaker you can get 30 minutes added to the 90 (tell them when you book the exam, not when you get to the test center, they can do nothing).

The exam is normally a few drags and drop, a few gimlets, and a good deal of single or multiple answer questions (The look/feel of the exam is in the link above).

Finally, you get a survey after you are done with the exam (before you get your results), I always have a hard time wanting to do it… I WANT MY RESULT NOW!
I try to, but if too impatient I just click next next next to get my results.

If you pass /high five, go celebrate.
If you fail, that sucks, sulk for a day or two then look at the report, it will show your weak/strong areas, go re-study the weak areas, and go back when you can (have to wait 5 working days).

Where do I learn CCNA? How do I get full preparation for the CCNA exam?
Profile photo for Doug White
Doug White
 · 
Follow
Professor, Cybersecurity and Networking at Roger Williams University (2003–present)Jan 24
Well, there are any number of ways:

Self Study — this would involve you buying a CCNA (check to ensure it is current) book and studying. You will need a book that provides you with packet tracer and the other materials required to prepare.
Assisted Study — using sites like Free CCNA Workbook | CCNA Labs, Practice Exams and More! or other study sites, can help you to prepare for very low cost.
A community college or other educational program near you (or online) — these can range from free to quite expensive and often are locked into old fashioned 15 week classes and such. But you may be surprised as well.
Bootcamps — quick, often expensive, and usually the best way to pass a cert. Spend a week focusing on the material, study up for a couple of days and take the test. These are available online.
Ensure that whatever you are using, you have practice tests and that you are consistently passing above 80% on the practice tests.

Grave Caution: When you are searching for any cert tests, be very, very careful, brain dump sites, and other scams abound with phishing links, malware, etc. So, as you search for answers, use extreme caution with sites promising free materials, answers, test questions etc.

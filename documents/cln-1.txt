What’s the best way to prepare for the CCNA? A Step-by-Step Guide
Cisco_CCNAPrep_720x188 

﻿For aspiring network professionals, the CCNA Certification is a critical step on the path to career growth and success. However, preparing for the 200-301 CCNA exam can feel intimidating, especially if you don’t know where to start. With the right training, resources, tools, and consistent effort, you can take on the exam with confidence. In this post, we’ll highlight a variety of training options, resources, and tools, along with practical tips to help you prepare effectively, ease exam anxiety, and succeed on game day.

 

What are the CCNA exam topics and why are they important?

When preparing, the CCNA exam topics are your most critical resource. Think of them as your guide to success that outlines exactly what you need to know to pass the exam. By leaning on these topics, you ensure your CCNA exam preparation remains focused and efficient, paving the way to successfully earning your certification. Let the exam topics be your guide as you navigate through your study and exam preparation.

Review the 200-301 CCNA v1.1 Exam Topics Now
 

What’s the best CCNA training to get started?

There are various training options available to help you prepare for the CCNA certification exam, catering to different preferences and budgets. Whether you prefer self-paced learning or a classroom setting, you can choose the option that best fits your learning style and needs:

Self-Study Cisco U. Learning Path: Implementing and Administering Cisco Solutions (CCNA): Ready to kick off your CCNA training journey? Gain hands-on skills to install, operate, configure, and verify basic networks while preparing for the 200-301 CCNA exam with over 37 hours of in-depth training, 31 interactive labs for practice, and 22 assessments to test and reinforce your knowledge.
Instructor-Led Course: Implementing and Administering Cisco Solutions (CCNA): Achieve success with this CCNA exam course, covering IPv4/IPv6 network setup, device configuration (switches, routers, WLAN controllers), network management, security basics, and the role of AI/ML in network operations.
CCNA Courses through the Cisco Networking Academy: The Cisco Networking Academy offers a three-course series to build your networking skills and get you ready for the CCNA certification and associate-level jobs.
CCNA 200-301 Official Cert Guide Library, 2nd Edition: Published by Cisco Press, these official self-study books provide a comprehensive review and practice for the latest CCNA exam. The library includes 2 volumes offering in-depth coverage and a realistic preparation experience.
 

What CCNA study materials and practice tools can help prepare for the exam?

In addition to official paid training, there are other resources, practice tools, and materials (many of them free) that can complement your core studies and deepen your understanding of the exam topics. Maximize your chances of success by leveraging a mix of resources to reinforce learning and address knowledge gaps effectively.

CCNA Certification Training Videos and CCNA Study Materials: Looking for more educational CCNA content? Enhance your CCNA preparation with free training videos and study materials. Access live webinar recordings by industry experts for in-depth insights into key exam topics, along with organized documents, recommended readings, and other resources to support your learning journey.
CCNA Prep Program: Register for this free program to access our on-demand series, where subject matter experts explore key topics for the 200-301 CCNA v1.1 exam. Enjoy engaging quiz questions, valuable resources, and plenty of opportunities to test your knowledge. Sign up now to stay informed about future live sessions and updates as well.
Cisco Packet Tracer: Sharpen your networking skills with Cisco Packet Tracer, a free simulator for CCNA exam prep. Access it via the Cisco Networking Academy by enrolling in the free "Introduction to Packet Tracer" course. Ready to start practicing? Download Packet Tracer today.
Bonus read: Using Packet Tracer for CCNA Study (with Sample Lab).
Cisco Modeling Labs - Personal: Simplify networking with this on-premises simulation tool for designing, testing, and troubleshooting Cisco and non-Cisco networks. Use real Cisco images to build reliable models faster and more affordably than physical labs. Purchase Cisco Modeling Labs - Personal.

Great tips! I passed the exam last month. Here’s a methodology I developed that worked for me. Hopefully you can take some of the parts that resonate with you.

 

Each sequence of steps (1-7) concludes with the ending of (1) chapter.

 

⁠Flash cards - this gets me familiar with what’s coming
CCNA official cert guide
Physical notebook (1st set of notes)
Jeremy IT videos
Google docs/ digitized notes (2nd set of notes - synthesized/ polished).
Enter CLI command steps learned from all chapters in one doc e.g. VLANS, STP, DHCP, RoaS, SSH/ Telnet, Layer 3 IPv4/ v6 addressing, etc
Packet Tracer - labs
Boson practice exams - you will get your teeth kicked in. These tests make the actual CCNA exam seem easier so it definitely makes you better prepared IMO. ( you can segment the exam by chapter)
 

Honorable mentions:

 

whenever I had time to kill (e.g. in a waiting room, on the train/ plane, lunch break, etc.…), I used a subnetting app called ‘subnetting practice master’. I personally struggled with subnetting but this app helped me big time.
noise cancellation ear buds are gold
 

Study hours

⁠2 hours per day work M-F
⁠4-6 hours per day Sat-Sun
 

Half the battle is developing a study system tailored to your learning style and being consistent. Make sure you delete all your social media apps. I’m serious!!! Turn ‘focus mode’ on in your phone. Sleep, diet, and gym is imperative. I can’t tell you how bad it was to retain information after a study session whenever i deviated from practicing this. Structure is important. All this will help set you up for your next certification endeavor.

 

Good luck!

I got my cert recently.

 

I did it with self study. I will not mention exact names or links because I am not making an "ad" here.

 

At first I tried with a CCNA bootcamp (N***l).

I advise this only for getting an idea.

If you already have some experience, I think you might skip this.

Don't get me wrong : it is OK and I still learned a lot, but for me it was not thorough enough on some points. And most of the same topics came back anyway in my second source.

 

The second source was the YT video's of J****y's IT labs, accompanied by his Anki flashcards. These are much more in detail.

And yes, on the exam you get questions about those "details" (examples hello timer, dead interval, . .) At least I had.

After all you have to prove that you know more than just having a general idea ;-)

 

Yes video's take a lot time. After each video make a resume of what exactly you have seen (for repeating and prepare the actual exam).

But some topics need time to sink in, so don't rush it !

 

And finally I did additional study, and practice exams on e***-p***.org.

The website has some questions that are not correct (I reported them), and they advice to use the app. The website can be used freely, the app has some limitations until you upgrade to "Pro" (and pay a small fee).

 

The videos of N******chuck also are very entertaining, accurate and helped me remembering things more easily. Yet it is only on some topics and you have to like the "energetic" style.

 

And finally : I didn't spend much time on labs. I know : shame on me 😥

But I think you end up learning more about the software for the labs, than actually getting knowledge on the exam topics.

Two things that I find is often overlooked when I chat to those planning their studies are the official exam blueprint, and the verbs used in the exam blueprint to describe what is required in a certain area of the test, and these verbs are a big indicator about how much knowledge is required by the student.

 

If I am asked to configure something in the exam, I know I need a deeper understanding in that area than if I am asked to describe something in another area within exam. The blueprint says what needs to be configured and what needs to be described, and everything else in between.

 

Here are two examples from the current blueprint. Reading through I know a greater level of understanding is required in the area of IPv4 addressing vs the area of REST-based APIs, because of the different verbs that are used:

- 6.5 Describe characteristics of REST-based APIs (authentication types, CRUD, HTTP verbs, and data encoding)

- 1.6 Configure and verify IPv4 addressing and subnetting

 

I never used the blueprint in my first CCNA because I wasn't aware it existed to be honest. I signed up to an online CCNA course and hoped for the best, which did work out okay thankfully, but now I always make sure the blueprint and verbs are well known, which helps to devise a solid study strategy.

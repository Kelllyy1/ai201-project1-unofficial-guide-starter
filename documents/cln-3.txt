How important is Packet Tracer to prepare for the CCNA exam?
I have completed my first PTTA, Packet Tracer Tutored Activity. I want to know how important this simulator is for preparing the final exam. Does the certification exam include Packet Tracer? Will I need to complete PTTA-like activities during the exam? 

 

Before posting here, I was told that the only job of the packet tracer is to practice network engineering in a virtual environment if one doesn't have real hardware. Is it correct?

Hello @ajbozdar​ ,

 

Packet Tracer may be important if it is your chosen method of practicing the material. It is a quick and convenient way to build virtual labs to practice and practice in general is very important to prepare for exams, as it allows you to solidify your theoretical knowledge. Of course, Packet Tracer is not the only option. You can use CML, GNS3, EVE-NG or real hardware for that purpose. Each comes with its pros and cons. The certification exam does not include any questions about Packet Tracer or its operation, but it may contain simlets, where you would be expected to perform practical configuration or troubleshooting elements. And to be comfortable with them, you definitely need some hands on experience, where Packet Tracer may help tremendously.

And regarding your last statement - yes, Packet Tracer is a simulator that helps you practice what network engineers do without investing in any real kit. Just like a flight simulator can teach you to fly a real plane without the need to buy one :-)

If you have never taken a Cisco certification exam, there is nothing like it. With any certification exam there are multiple versions of the exam and we never know which one we will take or which concentration of topics that will be covered.

 

I normally expose and complete practice exams from different vendors to help build a flexibilty to a certification exam. Some may say why take practice tests when you can take the real exam to see what it is like.

 

I took a Cisco course at a junior college. We used packet tracer. However, our final was a written test and a hands on assignment with real equipment. I found myself a little lost when I took the real equipment. I was imtimidated by the real equipment.

 

I did invest in refurbished economical equipment. In the process of setting up my lab with real equipment, I found there is so much to learn like how to use Putty, locating the right cables to connect to my Cisco switch and router. It takes time to absorb and learn networking with equipment.

 

So are you on a time crunch to pass the CCNA? Or do you have time to learn what you need for real world scenarios? This answer will determine your approach.

 

Packet Tracer is a great way to learn. You have to find what works for you and stick with it.

BEST STUDY METHODS FOR CCNA, These are my suggestions, please comment and I'll try to include yours as well.
Please like, comment, and share if you find this worthwhile. Thank you.

This is a little long, but worth it.   Please give a couple thumbs up and comment if find useful. Thanks


I have taught CCNA, or others, for about 25 years and have many certified successful students. 

The result: Be competent, be able to DO the job. Be Certified and QUALIFIED, be able to DO the job.


Be sure to reference the CCNA blueprint (exam topics) to follow along on your study plan:

https://learningnetwork.cisco.com/s/ccna-exam-topics 

 

1. I recommend guided learning thru a Cisco Networking Academy – if feasible

Excellent courses given by live instructor thru the Cisco Networking Academy available at community colleges, high schools, vocational colleges, universities, and non-profit organizations worldwide. You can find here: https://www.netacad.com/academy-locator

There are free course materials, labs, and Packet Tracer activities available at NetAcad.com
 This series from Wendell Odom on YouTube is excellent:  Wendell’sYouTube

Cisco U: https://u.cisco.com

ALSO

﻿Taking Cisco Learning Network’s “Study Groups” for discussion and troubleshooting practice.

Wendell Odom's video on YouTube: Video-based labs at YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLhDAmDsTz9XeObMAUL-vW-a9ESTb-O-Ay 

Wendell's https://www.certskills.com/config-labs/

 

2. Books – This absolutely the best book, in my opinion: I have used, and recommend, Wendell Odom’s book exclusively

Cisco Official Cert Guide Library (CCNA 200-301) by Wendell Odom 
The industry standard; comprehensive, detailed, with review questions and practice exams.
If you practice, practice, practice, you will learn the commands
 

3. Simulators and Labs – the best approach is to lab, lab, lab, configure, configure, configure, over and over until it is simple to do any command needed. Use the Packet Tracer (Free from Cisco) – it provides everything you need to master the CCNA material

Allows you to created your own networks and simulate network devices and topologies.
Download from the Cisco NetAcad website.
You can set up labs, save unconfigured, and configure many times to practice, this is an excellent approach to master the material
I suggest trying to simulate every diagram in your study text
By labbing, you build the neural muscles needed to do the job
GNS3 – awesome but more advanced and requires you to provide Cisco IOS file

More advanced, allows real Cisco IOS images (requires more resources).
Excellent for more complex topologies and real-world scenarios.
Boson is another simulator, not my favorite and has some cost to it, but worthwhile to mention.

LIVE GEAR - Setting up a home lab (even with a couple of old routers/switches or virtual devices) is awesome @Sadra​ 

 

Wendell Odom's labs: Written labs: www.certskills.com/config-labs 

 

Subnetting practice, as recommended by binary_whisperer473, there is a subnetting practice master app that you should consider for mastering subnetting practice, and subnettingquestions.com.

 

4. Online and digital resources

 Cisco Networking Academy – see above - NetAcad.com
Cisco Learning Network     https://learningnetwork.cisco.com
Cisco Learning Network Videos link
Cisco U: https://u.cisco.com
YouTube: Channels hosted by Wendell Odom, Jeremy Cioara, and Aaron Jayachandaran, and more, have very informative and useful videos.
CBT Nuggets source on Youtube or their own site. Jeremy, Keith and NetworkChuck.
Practice Tests

Some books have simulators and questions in the text, utilize them
Avoid “Brain Dumps,” as these will disqualify your certification and abilities
ExamCompass has free practice questions
Take as many practice tests as possible to familiarize yourself with how to take exams and determine relevant details in the questions, what are they really asking?
 

5. Study Methods

Structured Study Plan

Allocate a few months for preparation, depending on your experience and training.
Break down topics: Study one chapter at a time, master and move to the next, review
Daily schedule: 1-2 hours of study/lab practice. LAB, LAB, LAB, apply through practice labs
Hands-On Practice

Spend as much time as possible setting up topologies, configuring and practicing on simulators.
Design, build, configure, and troubleshoot your own network topologies in Packet Tracer or GNS3.
Practice, practice, practice, there is no substitute for quality practice
Practice Exams

Take practice exams after each topic to reinforce learning. conquer test anxiety
Simulate real exam conditions by using timed exams and no extra tools
Flashcards and Notes – buy online or create your own(, which is best)

Group Study and Discussion – find others to study with and share labs

Important/Critical

Focus on weak areas
Take practice exams
Master the “difficult” subjects like subnetting, ACL’s, network summarization (for multiarea OSPF), if you find it confusing/hard, put extra effort on that difficult/hard material, master it. A winner trains his weaknesses, a loser his strengths. Focus on your weaknesses and master them.
 

6. Exam Tips

Learn by repeated practicing, the Cisco CLI: be comfortable navigating and configuring routers and switches. Practice, practice, practice, there is not other way
Focus on subnetting using the “magic number” method from Wendell Odom’s certification guide: practice, repeatedly, until you’re fast and accurate. Be able to do many in your head
Read questions carefully to discern important, relevant details, learn to look out for keywords to identify specific details of the question
Finally

Stay consistent and study,then practice, hands-on, regularly, as much as you can, and take your time to understand. Understanding concepts deeply will help you not only pass the exam but ensure that you can actually DO the job.


Use ChatGPT to ask questions for immediate answers or other AI tools, Google, etc

@Shubert​  On this, every question is different so you have to be careful. Sometimes when you skim through a question, you think you know what they are asking. I'll repeat this below, Take the time to think(something we often dismiss), then work logically thru the answers to find the best one.

My suggestion, stop, reread it, read it again, and look for keywords that might provide guidance. Keywords can be NOT, except, most likely, and more. Once you have done that, evaluate each answer based upon what you believe they are looking for. You'll usually be able to eliminate a couple answers right away as red herrings. Then you have narrowed it down. Evaluate the remaining "possible" answers against your understanding of the question and chose the best one.

If the question involved subnetting or summarization, ACL configuration, double check everything.

As an example, if the question asks what first action a switch will do with a frame destined for an unknown MAC address, what are the keywords? First action and unknown MAC. What will a switch do first with that frame, use your brilliance with the basics to think thru the process of inbound frame handling. I.e. the switch just learned or relearned the source mac address(if that's an available answer), then since it's destination is unknown, what does a switch do with unknown destination frames - it floods it out all ports except the port that it was received on.

Take the time to think(something we often dismiss), then work logically thru the answers to find the best one. Don't grab the first likely answer you think is correct, go thru the process again.

On rare occasions, you might find two that seem to match, slow down and evaluate each carefully to see if one is better than the other.

I could go on, but I hope this helps. The more practice tests you do, the more example test questions you work thru, the more you will build test taking skills, and confidence.

At some point, once you've developed this skill, as you read books, you'll be able to create your own possible test questions, and likely might see some of them in the future. Good luck on your journey!!

@Bene​ There are several simulators you can utilize, but I have used, and highly recommend the Cisco Packet Tracer (link in attached post), as it is easy to setup and use, runs great, you can save partial configs, networks, total configs, run through lots of network designs lightning fast, it is really all you should need for CCNA.
